 Character Description
Taxonomy Family: Lycopodiaceae
Division: Pteridophyta
Class: Lycopodiopsida
Order: Lycopodiales
Genus: Lycopodiella
Species: Lycopodiella sp.
Stems Characterized by two systems: indeterminate horizontal rhizomes creeping on or just below the ground and rooting at intervals, and determinate erect lateral branches bearing strobili.
Leaves Small, simple, needle-like to narrowly triangular, spirally arranged and covering stems and branches; each leaf contains a single unbranched vascular strand.
Strobilus Found at terminal tips of upright branches; erect, nodding (pendulous), or sessile depending on species.
Habitat & Distribution
  • Moist, open, and often disturbed habitats.
  • Wet sandy or peaty soils such as bogs, swamps, pond margins, and pine flatwoods.
Distribution: Cosmopolitan in tropical and temperate regions.
Beneficial Plant Part No Plant Part Uses
1 Whole plant Used to treat rheumatism, hepatitis, dysentery, stomach ulcers, hypertension, gout, arthritis, coughs, and asthma.
2 Leaves Used to filter palm wine and as a natural insect repellent against fleas and cockroaches.

  • Moist lowland forests and forest margins
  • Riverbanks, swampy areas, and wet shaded slopes
  • Often found on acidic to neutral soil, with high organic content
Distribution: Southeast Asia, including Malaysia, Indonesia, Singapore, Thailand, Papua New Guinea, and northern Australia
Beneficial Plant Part No Plant Part Uses
1 Young fronds (leaves) Edible when young; consumed as a vegetable in some regions.
2 Rhizome Used in traditional medicine in some cultures for fever, skin, ailments, and wound healing.
3 Whole plant Sometimes used as ornamental due to palm-like appearance.

 Character Description
Taxonomy Family: Polypodiaceae
Division: Pteridophyta
Class: Polypodiopsida
Order: Polypodiales
Genus: Phymatosorus
Species: Phymatosorus sp.
Rhizome Long-creeping, slender to moderately thick, greenish to brown, covered with pale to dark brown, lanceolate scales; produces spreading coloni.
Fronds Simple, entire, or shallowly lobed; leathery texture; often shiny on upper surface.
Petiole Short, stout, slightly winged; often pale brown to green.
Sori Round, scattered, raised “wart-like” sori on the underside of fronds; without indusium; arranged irregularly (distinctive identification feature).
Habitat & Distribution
  • Epiphytic on tree trunks, branches, fallen logs
  • Lithophytic on rocks in moist forests
  • Occasionally terrestrial on rich, organic soil
  • Occurs in lowland to lower montane forest
  • Thrives in moist, well-drained areas with moderate air circulation
Distribution: Widely found in Southeast Asia, including Malaysia, Indonesia, Singapore, Thailand, the Philippines, Papua New Guinea, Polynesia, Micronesia, and northern Australia.
Beneficial Plant Part No Plant Part Uses
1 leaves fronds Used in traditional medicine as poultices for skin inflammation, wounds, fever reduction, and body aches.
2 Rhizome Sometimes used in herbal infusions or decoctions for cough, flu, and general body weakness.
3 Whole plant Popular ornamental for gardens and indoor decoration due to attractive fronds and ability to grow on pots, trees, or hanging arrangements.
4 Dried fronds Used in some places for decorative floral crafts.

 Character Description
Taxonomy Family: Lygodiaceae
Division: Pteridophyta
Class: Polypodiopsida
Order: Polypodiales
Genus: Lygodium
Species: Lygodium sp.
Rhizome Creeping, slender, covered with fine brownish scales; produces wiry climbing fronds.
Fronds Indeterminate and twining; rachis functions like a vine stem, allowing the fern to climb shrubs or small trees. Pinnules are opposite or alternate, small, ovate to lanceolate.
Rachis Green, wiry, flexible; capable of coiling and supporting the plant as it climbs.
Sori Found along the margins of fertile pinnules, often protected by reflexed leaf margins forming a false indusium.
Habitat & Distribution
  • Common in moist secondary forests, roadside vegetation, and open scrublands.
  • Prefers well-drained soil and partially shaded to open sunlight.
  • Often found climbing over shrubs, fences, or other plants.
Distribution: Tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. In Malaysia, commonly found in lowland forest edges and disturbed areas.
Beneficial Plant Part No Plant Part Uses
1 leaves fronds Eaten as a vegetable (“paku resam” in local cuisine). Rich in vitamins and minerals.
2 Fonds and Rhizome Sometimes boiled for herbal bath or decoction to treat body pain.
3 Whole plant Used in traditional medicine for fever, skin rashes, and inflammation.

 Character Description
Taxonomy Family: Pteridaceae
Division: Pteridophyta
Class: Polypodiopsida
Order: Polypodiales
Genus: Adiantum
Species: Adiantum sp.
Common name Maidenhair fern
Rhizome Creeping, slender, dark brown to black, covered with fine scales.
Fronds Delicate, arching; compound with many small fan-shaped pinnules attached to black wiry stalks.
Rachis and Stipe Glossy black or dark purplish-brown, slender, hair-like.
Sori Found along the outer margins of pinnules; protected by reflexed lobes of the lamina acting as a false indusium.
Texture Leaflets thin and light green, with distinctive airy appearance.
Habitat & Distribution
  • Moist, shaded areas such as stream banks, waterfalls, limestone walls, and forest undergrowth.
  • Prefers high humidity and indirect light.
Distribution: Pantropical distribution — found in Southeast Asia, South America, and Africa. In Malaysia, often cultivated ornamentally and found in humid forest edges.
Beneficial Plant Part No Plant Part Uses
1 leaves fronds Sometimes used in cosmetic and hair care products for shine and strength.
2 whole plant Used traditionally for respiratory ailments, cough, and fever.
3 ornamental Popular as indoor and landscape fern due to elegant fronds.

 Character Description
Taxonomy Family: Nephrolepidaceae
Division: Pteridophyta
Class: Polypodiopsida
Order: Polypodiales
Genus: Nephrolepis
Species: Nephrolepis sp.
Common name Paku pedang
Rhizome Creeping to short-erect, covered with brown to dark brown scales. It produces numerous adventitious roots and stolon-like runners that aid in vegetative propagation
Fronds Simple-pinnate, long, arching, and tufted, arising from the rhizome ; bright green, linear-lanceolate in shape
Rachis and Stipe Short to moderately long, pale green to brown.
Sori Round to slightly elongated, arranged in a single row on each side of the midrib of the pinnae.
Habitat & Distribution
  • Grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
  • Terrestrial, epiphytic, or lithophytic fern.
  • Found in riverbanks, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
Distribution: Tropical and subtropical regions, Southeast Asia, including Malaysia, Thailand, and Indonesia. Also found in Africa, Australia, the Pacific Islands, and the Americas.
No Plant Part Uses
1 leaves fronds Used as ornamental plants in gardens, homes, and landscaping due to their attractive, arching foliage.
2 Rhizome Traditionally used in folk medicine for treating coughs, wounds, and skin ailments
3 whole plant Acts as a natural air purifier, improving indoor air quality by removing toxins.

Class: Polypodiopsida
Order: Polypodiales
Genus: Pyrrosia

Common name Paku sisik naga
Rhizome Creeping, densely covered with brown to golden hairs or scales; gives rise to tufted fronds
Fronds Simple, entire, leathery, and sometimes succulent; may be small to medium-sized, often covered with hairs on the lower surface.
Rachis and Stipe Stipe short or inconspicuous; rachis slender, usually hairy or scaly along its length.
Sori Round, arranged along the veins on the underside of fronds; usually without an indusium.
Habitat & Distribution
  • Mostly epiphytic, growing on tree trunks or rocks; sometimes terrestrial.
  • Found in moist, shaded forests or tropical/subtropical regions.
  • Humid environments, often in lowland to montane areas.
Distribution: Widely distributed in tropical and subtropical Asia, including Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, and the Philippines. Found in parts of Africa, Australia, and the Pacific Islands.
No Plant Part Uses
1 leaves fronds Used in traditional medicine for treating wounds, burns, and skin infections.
2 whole plant Sometimes used as an ornamental fern for indoor decoration or garden landscaping.

 Character Description
Taxonomy Family: Lycopodiaceae
Division: Lycopodiophyta
Class: Lycopodiopsida
Order: Lycopodiales
Genus: Huperzia
Species: Huperzia sp.
Common name Fir clubmoss
Rhizome Creeping, branched, and covered with small, scale-like leaves; produces dense tufts or mats.
Fronds Small, linear to lanceolate, sessile, and tough; arranged spirally along the stem; not pinnate like typical ferns.
Rachis and Stipe The stipe is very short or almost absent; the rachis is continuous with the stem, with leaves spirally arranged.
Sori Sporangia are borne in the axils of specialized sporophylls at the tip of the stem, forming strobili (cone-like structures); not on the underside of leaves like typical ferns.
Habitat & Distribution
  • Grows in moist, shaded forests, often on soil, rocks, or as epiphytes on trees.
  • Prefers cool, humid environments, typically in montane or submontane regions.
  • Found in areas with high humidity and low light intensity.
Distribution: Widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions, including Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia). Also found in South America, Africa, and parts of Oceania. Usually occurs in highland and montane forest ecosystems.
No Plant Part Uses
1 Sporophytes Source of Huperzine A, a compound studied for cognitive enhancement and Alzheimer’s disease.
2 whole plant Used in traditional medicine to treat inflammation, fever, and memory-related disorders.

Taxonomy Family: Davalliaceae
Division: Pteridophyta
Class: Polypodiopsida
Order: Polypodiales
Genus: Davallia
Species: Davallia sp.
Common name Rabbit’s foot fern
Rhizome Creeping, thick, and densely covered with brown to silvery hairy scales, often growing above the soil surface (distinctive “rabbit’s foot” appearance).
Fronds Triangular to broadly ovate; 2–4 pinnate, finely divided, light to bright green.
Rachis and Stipe Slender, brown to dark brown, scaly near the base.
Sori Small, round, located near the margins of pinnules; protected by cup-shaped indusia formed from reflexed leaf margins.
Habitat & Distribution
  • Epiphytic or terrestrial
  • Found on tree trunks, rocks, and forest floors
  • Prefers moist, shaded, well-drained environments
  • Common in lowland and hill forests
Distribution: Tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, especially Southeast Asia including Malaysia.
No Plant Part Uses
1 Rhizome Used in traditional medicine for treating inflammation, wounds, and minor skin infections.
2 whole plant Widely cultivated as an ornamental fern due to its unique furry rhizomes and delicate fronds.

Division: Pteridophyta
Class: Polypodiopsida
Order: Gleicheniales
Genus: Dicranopteris
Species: Dicranopteris linearis
Common name Resam fern
Rhizome Creeping rhizomes that grow horizontally on or just below the soil surface, spread extensively and form dense colonies
Fronds dichotomously branched, giving a repeated Y-shaped (forked) appearance, fronds are erect to arching and can reach up to 2–4 m in length, frond surface is rough to the touch, and the underside bears sporangia

Habitat & Distribution

  • roadsides, hillslopes, forest edges, logged areas, abandoned agricultural land, and secondary forests
  • prefers well-drained, acidic, nutrient-poor soils and thrives under high light conditions
Distribution: Widely distributed in Southeast Asia, including Malaysia, Indonesia, the Philippines, and northern Australia.
No Plant Part Uses

2 Rhizome Help stabilise soil, Contribute to slope protection.

Taxonomy Family: Selaginellaceae
Division: Lycopodiophyta
Class: Selaginellopsida
Order: Selaginellales
Genus: Selaginella
Species: Selaginella sp.
Common name Spike moss
Leaves microphylls (small leaves with a single unbranched vein), leaf size unequal (anisophyllous), leaves scale-like, arranged in four rows along the stem, Lacks true fronds and sori (distinguishes it from true ferns)
Rhizophores eafless structures that produce roots
Sporangea heterosporous (produce two types of spores: megaspores and microspores), Spores borne in compact cone-like structures called strobili at stem tips
Habitat & Distribution
  • forest floors, shaded moist areas, rocks, tree bases, and even deserts (the “resurrection plant”)
Distribution: Very wide global distribution, especially tropical & subtropical regions; some species extend into temperate zones
No Plant Part Uses
1 Whole Plant Traditional medicine: Some species (S. tamariscina, S. bryopteris) used for anti-inflammatory, antioxidant properties, Used as ornamental ground cover in gardens and terrariums

Taxonomy Family: Polypodiaceae
Division: Pteridophyta
Class: Polypodiopsida
Order: Polypodiales
Genus: Platycerium
Species: Platycerium sp.
Common name Staghorn fern
Rhizome short and creeping, anchoring the plant to host surface.
Sori large, brown, patch-like, located on underside of fertile fronds, No indusium covering the sori
Sterile (Basal) Fronds Rounded, shield-shaped, Overlap and press against the substrate, Brown when mature, Function to protect roots and trap organic debris
Fertile Fronds Long, green, forked repeatedly, Antler- or stag-horn shaped, Bear reproductive structures
Habitat & Distribution
  • Epiphytic fern growing attached to tree trunks or branches, non-parasitic
  • Found in tropical and subtropical rainforests
  • Prefers high humidity and good air circulation
  • Grows in shaded to semi-shaded forest canopies
Distribution: Native to tropical & subtropical rainforests of Africa, Southeast Asia, Australia, New Guinea, Pacific islands
No Plant Part Uses
1 Fertile Fronds Primary decorative feature used in ornamental displays, Improve aesthetic and economic value in horticulture
2 Sterile (Basal) Fronds Trap rainwater, leaf litter, and dust, Contribute to nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems, Create microhabitats for small organisms
3 Whole Plant Popular ornamental plant (mounted on wood or walls), urban greening and eco-friendly landscaping, Supports canopy biodiversity, Enhances forest nutrient retention
